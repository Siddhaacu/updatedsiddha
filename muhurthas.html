Planets (Grahas)

Sun (Surya)

Associated with ego, sense of purpose, and vitality. Son of Aditi and Kashyapa; Surya means “the supreme light.”

Moon (Chandra)

Associated with emotions, the mind, the mother, and nurturing instincts. Chandra means “shining.”

Mars (Kuja / Mangal)

Associated with energy, courage, discipline, conflict, and physical strength. Often linked with warriors, initiative, and decisive action.

Mercury (Budha)

Associated with communication, intellect, wit, and adaptability. A planet-god known for preserving and protecting nature, and governing speech, learning, and cleverness.

Jupiter (Guru / Brihaspati)

Mentor and teacher of the Devas (gods), always supporting them in battles against Asuras. Guru means “teacher” or “priest”; Brihaspati means “lord of prayer or devotion.” Associated with wisdom, dharma, blessings, and expansion.

Venus (Shukra)

The mentor of Asuras (demons). Associated with fertility, beauty, art, luxury, and enthusiasm. Shukra means “clear, pure, brightness, or clearness.”

Saturn (Shani)

The planet of karma and justice, giving the fruits of one’s deeds. Associated with discipline, responsibility, status, patience, wisdom, and endurance — but also hardships and delays.

Rahu

Represents materialism, desire, obsession, mischief, fear, and confusion. In mythology, Rahu is a shadow planet and an enemy to the Sun and Moon, causing eclipses.

Ketu

Represents karmic collections (both good and bad), spirituality, moksha, and subtle or supernatural influences. Often connected with detachment and inner enlightenment.
