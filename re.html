Quick reference for weekdays (Vara), lunar tithis, months (Masa), and the 60-year cycle used in traditional Hindu calendars.

Vara (Weekdays)

In the Hindu Panchanga a day runs from sunrise to the next sunrise (not midnight to midnight). Below are short notes on each vara and traditional guidance on activities.

Sunday — Adivaram (Sun / Aditya)

Ruled by Surya. Day of vitality, leadership, integrity. Good for activities connected to nature, fire, coronation, gold, and public life. Avoid arrogance and impatience.

Monday — Somavaram (Moon / Chandra)

Ruled by Chandra. Day of intuition, creativity, and mother-related matters. Good for weddings, buying clothes, and nurturing tasks. Avoid rigidity and cosmetic procedures.

Tuesday — Mangalavaram (Mars / Mangal)

Ruled by Mangal. Practical day for material work, metallurgy, medicine, and physical tasks. Avoid starting delicate ventures or unnecessary travel; be cautious of injuries.

Wednesday — Budhavaram (Mercury / Budha)

Ruled by Budha. Great for communication, trade, learning, media, publishing and starting refined projects. Avoid deceit and gambling on this day.

Thursday — Guruvaram (Jupiter / Brihaspati)

Ruled by Brihaspati. Very auspicious for learning, charity, marriage, financial decisions, and spiritual practice. Meet teachers and elders on this day where possible.

Friday — Shukravaram (Venus / Shukra)

Ruled by Shukra. Suited for love, arts, beauty, luxury purchases, and social gatherings. Avoid solitary or secretive transactions.

Saturday — Shanivaram (Saturn / Shani)

Ruled by Shani. A day for rest, discipline, charity and long-term planning. Avoid starting risky ventures; focus on recovery and practical work.

Tithis (Lunar Days)

There are 15 tithis in each waxing and waning phase (total 30 distinct tithi names). Each tithi has traditional qualities and suitability for particular activities.

Short Tithi guide (Pratipada → Purnima / Amavasya)

1. Pratipada — auspicious for beginnings and ceremonies.

2. Dwitiya — good for starting tasks and laying foundations.

3. Tritiya — victory, suitable for important starts and rituals.

4. Chaturthi — traditionally Rikta; suitable for removal of obstacles, not for major starts.

5. Panchami — healing, completing tasks, auspicious works.

6. Shasthi — favorable for coronation, construction, jewelry work.

7. Saptami — good for journeys, events, and decoration.

8. Ashtami — martial/competitive activities and important undertakings.

9. Navami — Rikta; used historically for competitive or forceful tasks.

10. Dashami — completion and virtuous acts; good for ceremonies and journeys.

11. Ekadasi — fasting, worship, festivals and vows.

12. Dvadasi — auspicious for rituals and duty; avoid some journeys and baths in certain traditions.

13. Trayodasi — auspicious for Shiva worship (Pradosha) and many ceremonies.

14. Chaturdasi — Rikta; generally used for routine works and avoiding new ventures.

15. Purnima / Amavasya — Full / New Moon days: Purnima is auspicious for beginnings & completion; Amavasya is often reserved for rites, ancestral ceremonies and introspective work.

Masa (Months) & Seasons

Two month-count systems are used in India: Amanta (month begins after new moon — common in the south) and Purnimanta (month begins after full moon — common in the north). Festivals align across systems.

Seasons & month examples

Six seasons (Rtu) and twelve months such as: Chaitra (Vasanta), Vaisakha (Vasanta), Jyeshta (Grishma), Ashadha (Grishma), Shravan (Varsha), Bhadrapada (Varsha), Ashwija/Kartika (Sharada), Margashira/Pushya (Hemanta), Magha/Phalguna (Shishira), etc.

Adhika Masam (Extra month) & Kshaya Masam (Lost month)

Adhika Masam: An extra lunar month added occasionally (7 times in 19 years) to sync lunar months with the solar year.

Kshaya Masam: Rare; occurs when the sun transits two rashis inside a single lunar month — the month is labeled by both transits (a 'lost' month).

Sixty-year Cycle (Samvatsara)

Names of the 60 years (short)

The sixty names are used in traditional calendars (e.g., Prabhava, Vibhava, Shukla, Pramodyuta, … Akshaya). These cycle every 60 years and are used to reference years in many regional calendars (Ugadi, etc.).

Quick Tips

  • Vara selection — prefer days that match the nature of the activity (e.g., Guruvaram for learning, Shukravaram for arts).
  • Tithi caution — avoid Rikta tithis for starting major, long-term projects.
  • Adhika months — acts during adhika masa are performed with traditional adjustments; consult a priest or astrologer for rituals.
