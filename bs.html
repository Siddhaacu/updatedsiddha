What are Nakshatras?

Nakshatras are lunar mansions — 27 equal divisions of the ecliptic widely used in Vedic astrology. Each Nakshatra spans 13°20' and is further divided into four padas (quarters). The Moon moves roughly one Nakshatra per day.

Historically 28 asterisms were known; the classical list of 27 appears in the Vedanga Jyotisha and later astronomical texts such as the Surya Siddhanta. Nakshatras are used for muhurtha (timing) decisions, naming syllables, and dasha (planetary period) calculation.

Lords cycle: Ketu (7 years) → Venus (20 years) → Sun (6 years) → Moon (10 years) → Mars (7 years) → Rahu (18 years) → Jupiter (16 years) → Saturn (19 years) → Mercury(17 years) (repeats).

Classification & Practical Uses

Classical nakshatras are grouped by nature: Fixed, Soft (Gentle), Light (Swift), Dreadful, Temporary (Movable), Fierce (Cruel) and Mixed. These classifications are useful for timing activities such as building, ceremonies, journeys, arts, or military operations.

  • Fixed — Rohini, Uttaraphalguni, Uttarasadha, Uttarabhadrapada. Good for long-lasting works.
  • Soft/Gentle — Mrigasira, Chitra, Anuradha, Revati. Good for arts, friendships, and rituals.
  • Light/Swift — Ashvini, Pusya, Hasta. Good for travel, starting short tasks, medical treatment.
  • Dreadful — Arudra, Aslesa, Jyeshta, Mula. Associated with intense/transformative actions.
  • Temporary/Movable — Purnavasu, Swati, Shravana, Dhanishta, Satabhisha. Good for temporary tasks and travel.
  • Fierce / Cruel — Bharani, Magha, Purva Phalguni, Purva Ashadha, Purva Bhadrapada. Use caution; often connected to destructive activity.

How to use Nakshatras in practice

Use nakshatra nature to select auspicious timings for events (muhurtha), choose naming syllables, and interpret dasha periods. For delicate ceremonies pick gentle or fixed stars; for travel and short actions pick swift or movable stars.
